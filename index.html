Cities of the Dead Remix Project

We were assigned to combine some element of modern culture with excerpts from Italo Calvini’s book Invisible Cities. In Invisible Cities, Marco Polo tells Kublai Khan of the numerable cities of Khan's expansive empire. He describes his “travels” by poetically describing 55 unique takes on the City of Venice. Of the 55, the Cities of the Dead depict dark and horrific living quarters.

To alter the narration by Marco Polo, I used algorithms to analyze dialogue pulled from the caption files of the Netflix series Stranger Things. The analysis gave me the most repeated phrases from every episode of Stranger Things. Each episode was then matched one of the Cities of the Dead based on theme and episode title.

The repeated phrases were inserted into the text of Invisible Cities to create a new narration and highlight common themes and fears that have persisted through the years in the horror genre.

At a high-art level, this project was about creating a symbol of Campbellian storytelling, and drawing attention to it by not only repeating the stories of Italo Calvini, but with the processes used to combine these stories.

At a more pragmatic level, it was about narrative, creating a consistent style, and learning about book design.
